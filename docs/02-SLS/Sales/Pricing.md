## Pricing Philosophy

SLS pricing is built on four principles:

**1. Price to value, not to cost.**
The price of a service reflects what it saves or earns for the client — not how long it took to build. A tool that saves $2,000/month in labor and freight is worth $500/month regardless of whether it took two weeks or two months to build.

**2. Use the value equation as the framework.**
*(Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice) = Value*

- **Dream Outcome:** Always lead with financial impact — hours saved, freight costs avoided, billing errors caught, revenue protected. Quantify it.
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement:** The weakest variable right now. Fixed by getting wins, documenting outcomes, and building case studies. This is why early pricing is conservative — buying proof, not undervaluing the product.
- **Time Delay:** SLS's strongest differentiator. No IT involvement, no ERP replacement, no 6-month implementation. Clients go live in weeks. This should be stated explicitly in every sales conversation.
- **Effort & Sacrifice:** "Send me a CSV" is the ask. Activation energy for the client is nearly zero. No new software to learn, no integrations to configure, no training sessions.

**3. Price phases progressively.**
Phase 1 is priced to get a foot in the door and deliver proof. Phase 2 and beyond are priced at full value. By the time a client reaches Phase 2, SLS has already demonstrated results — the perceived likelihood of achievement is established and the discount is no longer needed.

**4. Build toward premium positioning.**
The long-term target is to be priced at or above ERP module competitors ($500–2,000/month) while remaining far easier to implement and more directly valuable. That positioning requires a track record. It's being built now.

## Pricing Evolution Roadmap

### Stage 1 — Now (Pre-revenue, building first client)
- Phase 1 at $175/mo intentionally underpriced
- Goal: Get a live client, document the before/after
- Outcome needed: "Blue Ash went from 5 hours of manual reconciliation to 15 minutes"

### Stage 2 — First 2–3 Clients
- Phase 1 stays at $175/mo for new clients (still building case studies)
- Phase 2 and 3 at full price — earned through Phase 1 delivery
- Goal: 2–3 different client environments, 2–3 documented outcomes
- Outcome needed: Pattern recognition — same tool working across different systems/industries

### Stage 3 — 5+ Clients
- Phase 1 price increases to $250–300/mo
- All phases at or above current full pricing
- Begin building waitlist positioning — scarcity is real at this point
- Goal: Case studies published on website, reference clients available

### Stage 4 — 10+ Clients
- Full premium positioning
- JFM Starter at $400/mo, reconciliation at $350/mo, PO Tracking at $700/mo
- Competing directly with ERP module pricing but winning on speed and simplicity
- Goal: Price is no longer a question — outcome and ease of implementation are the sell

*SLS's price should always sit below ERP module pricing but above "DIY is fine." The sell is: cheaper than the software that requires IT, better than the spreadsheet that costs you hours.*

## Value Documentation (Track for Each Client)

For every client and every phase, capture:

1. **Baseline time cost:** How many hours/month was the manual process taking?
2. **Baseline error rate:** How many mismatches, delays, or missed items per month?
3. **Baseline cash cost:** Expedited freight, billing disputes, late fees, etc.
4. **Post-SLS time:** How long does the same process take now?
5. **Post-SLS catches:** How many real issues did the tool flag in month 1?
6. **Client quote:** Get a direct quote from Travis (or whoever) about what changed

These six data points are the sales asset for every future prospect conversation.

## Notes on Hourly Rate

Additional oversight is billed at **$175/hour.**

This rate should not be discounted. It reflects:
- Systems analyst-level expertise
- Manufacturing operations domain knowledge
- Real-time problem-solving, not templated advice

If a client pushes back on the hourly rate, the answer is: "Most consultants in this space charge $200–300/hour. I charge $175 because I want the relationship to be sustainable for both of us — but the rate reflects the expertise."
